Dr. Norman Borlaug was an American agronomist and humanitarian who led initiatives to improve agricultural productivity around the world. He is often credited with saving a billion people from starvation due to his work on developing high-yielding wheat varieties.

Dr. Borlaug was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970 for his contributions to world peace through increasing food supply.
